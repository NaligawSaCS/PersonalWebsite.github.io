Autobiography

My name is Ian Emmanuel Comia, I was raised in a relatively small town of Taysan which is in the province of Batangas where I still currently reside. I was born on March 10, 2005. And some things I can say about myself is that I’m very strict with time, I hate confrontations, I want to live an extremely simple, comfortable and stress free life, I guess I handle problems extremely well, and I’m not afraid to fail but I’m afraid of being a disappointment.

My life is extremely simple and easy to live. I thank my parents for that, they provide me with everything I could ever need and more. When I was a kid, I enjoyed the smallest things in life like a gun shaped piece of wood or a sword-like looking stick. Looking back at it, I was really easy to please as a child. Then I developed a fascination with music and thanks to my parents I was able to have music lessons. I can say that I'm a little proficient with reading sheet music. Then in the late years of my elementary education, I developed an interest in photography and the technical aspects related to media creation.

In 2016, I was able to qualify at the Division Schools Press Conference as a technical director in the Radio Broadcasting Contest. Which I guess became the start of my interest in computers since I kept on searching for more technical information in what I was doing. This interest in technical things kept going during my junior highschool years. I joined a camera and ICT club at my previous school. Then my senior highschool year was no different. I still enjoyed taking pictures, I loved editing photos and videos, I also started learning about programming. Which led to the two college courses I had to choose. It was between Multimedia Arts or Computer Science. I obviously chose Computer Science, and I have no regrets, but sometimes I do wonder what would have happened if I chose the latter. And here I am now, enjoying the early days of my college life as a Computer Science student. I know that this is just the beginning of an extremely stressful and difficult program that I hope would someday lead to a life that I very much enjoy.
